Problem

Scheduling health and wellness care appointments is complex. So is tracking down important health history. Many people drop off from scheduling an appointment mid-process due to frustration. How might we empower users to be more proactive with their health and wellness care through easier access to what they need?

Goal

To simplify the process of finding care including everything from in-network healthcare to more nontraditional wellness care through a mobile app.

Role

UI/UX Designer and Strategist

Client

CareerFoundry UX Immersion Course

Timeline

5 Months

Tools Used

  • Adobe Xd
  • Usability Hub
  • Optimal Workshop

Insights

While talking with a nurse practitioner, I learned about some struggles in the patient-caregiver relationship. Most of them related to patients being disconnected from their healthcare. I then decided complete interviews to determine if healthcare patients agreed that they were disconnected. This seemed to be overwhelmingly agreed upon. For my next step. I analyzed the existing health app market to determine if there were any apps that could help patients be more engaged with their health. It became obvious there was an opportunity for a better health charting app in the market.

Hypothesis

By enabling users to schedule health and wellness appointments, log & share health data and log personal health goals, we will see them more engaged with their health and wellness.

Interviews

Six people who already used at least one health or wellness app were interviewed. Insights are described below.

Our Persona, Imani

As a millennial, Imani is motivated by opportunities to make obligations such as healthcare easier through technology. His barriers to use are not having time & motivation for app use amongst a busy & distracted life. The features most important to him are:

  • 1. Searching for a provider - Filter by date, location, insurance or specialization. Holistic and non-traditional care should be available too.
  • 2. Contacting a provider via messaging - Though phone calls will likely also be an option, people like having multiple avenues to reach out to a provider.
  • 3. Health data storage - A simple and clean system to store data, whether sent by care provider or input by user, is critical.

User Flows

The architecture of the app starts to come together via in-depth user flows.

Wireframes

Beginning with sketches and iterating gradually to higher fidelity, select flows of the app start to be built for screen.

User Testing

With the high fidelity prototype ready to go, six willing participants were invited to complete a usability test and the prototype was opened up to feedback. The errors and negative feedback were categorized, quantified and rated by severity in order to prioritize necessary changes.
